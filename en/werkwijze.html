Sheet 03 — How we work

How it works with us, from your first invoice to your last tax return.

No surprises, no fine print: this is exactly the story we also tell on the phone to everyone who wants to become a client.

A — Bookkeeping

First, we get the foundation right.

A·1

Just started?

You've just started as an entrepreneur and want to double-check where to begin. First of all: congratulations on founding your business! We'll help you with all the administrative and tax obligations.

A·2

We work with SnelStart

You don't have to create an account yourself; we do that together during the intake call.

Been running your business for a while, on different software? Then check with us what makes sense. We have experience with most major packages, such as Exact and the former Unit4, but also with smaller ones like Moneybird. Switching is usually no problem. And is keeping your current package important to you? Then we're happy to talk it through.

A·3

Sales invoices

Once your SnelStart account is set up, it's time for your first sales invoice. You create and send it via the SnelStart website or the app. We'll show you how it works.

A·4

Purchase invoices

Meanwhile, you're also incurring costs. More than you think! See Cost structure under Tips & tricks. The PDFs you receive by e-mail are easily uploaded via the SnelStart website under Purchases. Get a receipt in a shop? Take a photo right away with the SnelStart app and throw it out. Easy. Just always check that the photo is sharp.

A·5

Linking your bank account

During the intake call we link your business bank accounts together. Note: we can only link business accounts, not private accounts you use for business. Also check whether SnelStart supports your bank (see snelstart.nl/bankkoppelingen).

With the link active, incoming payments are automatically matched against your outstanding invoices, so you always have an up-to-date debtor overview. The same goes for outgoing payments and the receipts and invoices you've uploaded.

B — Tax returns

You stay in control. Always.

Pick your legal form and see exactly which returns apply to you.

Default view: sole trader / VOF

B·1

VAT (btw/OB): for everyone

In the first week of the new quarter, you make sure your administration is up to date: all receipts uploaded in the Purchases portal. We then process your administration and prepare the draft VAT return. We e-mail that draft to you, so you can go through it. Do you approve? Then you send us an explicit request to file, and we file it for you. That way you keep a grip on your own taxes and stay in control of what goes to the Dutch tax authorities.

B·2

Payroll taxes: if you have staff or are on the payroll yourself

If you're on your own BV's payroll or you employ staff, payroll taxes come into play. We make sure a payroll tax number is applied for. You tell us what you or your employee will earn, whether holiday pay is paid out, travel allowance, and so on.

A payslip is produced every month and the payroll tax return is taken care of; you receive copies of both by e-mail. You also get two payment files each month to import into your bank: one for the salaries, one for the payroll taxes. The right amounts then go to the right accounts, on the day you specify. So you no longer have to think about it, and everything is paid neatly on time.

B·3

Income tax / business return: sole traders, VOFs and similar

Once we've filed the four VAT returns, we've reached the end of the year. You're scheduled in for closing the books and preparing the draft annual accounts. We also ask you for the information and documents needed for your income tax return (see our Income tax checklist under Tips & tricks).

As soon as we have everything, we prepare the income tax return. You receive the drafts by e-mail so you can review everything calmly, and we immediately schedule a call to discuss them: both the draft annual accounts of your business and the draft income tax return. Do you approve? Then you e-mail us the explicit request to file based on the discussed drafts, and we file them for you. With that, your private filing obligations are also fulfilled.

B·3

Corporate income tax (VPB): BVs and similar only

Once we've filed the four VAT returns, we've reached the end of the year. You're scheduled in for closing the books and preparing the draft annual accounts. At the same time we prepare a draft of the corporate income tax return. We discuss both drafts with you, and after your approval we file them. With that, all your business filing obligations are fulfilled. All that remains is your personal income tax.

B·4

Income tax after the VPB

Once your BV's annual accounts are done, we get to work on your personal income tax. We ask you for the necessary information and documents (see our Income tax checklist under Tips & tricks), and as soon as we have them, we prepare the return. You receive the draft by e-mail, and we immediately schedule a call to discuss the documents — often in one go, together with the draft annual accounts and the draft corporate income tax return. Do you approve? Then you explicitly request us by e-mail to file the returns based on the discussed drafts, and we submit them for you. With that, your private filing obligations are also fulfilled.

One fixed rule

One fixed rule for all your tax returns

Whether it's VAT, corporate income tax or income tax: we only file after you explicitly request us by e-mail to file based on the discussed drafts. It sounds formal, but it's your safeguard: nothing ever goes to the Dutch tax authorities without you knowing exactly what's in it.

Only the monthly payroll tax returns fall outside this rule; those are taken care of automatically based on the agreements you've made with us.

C — Annual planning

When can you expect what?

VAT month (VAT return) ongoing work
  • JanuaryVAT month: updating administrations and filing VAT returns.
  • February and MarchWe finish the previous year's tax returns.
  • AprilVAT month.
  • May and JunePreparing for the new year's tax returns. In this period we also often handle urgent requests, such as figures for a mortgage application.
  • JulyVAT month.
  • August and SeptemberIn full swing closing administrations and preparing income tax and corporate income tax returns.
  • OctoberVAT month.
  • November and DecemberWe keep working on administrations and finishing income tax and corporate income tax. This is also when year-end questions come in more often. For example: if I want to stop my business, is it smart to do so before the turn of the year?

So keep in mind that we file the first tax returns at the end of May / beginning of June at the earliest, and that in February/March of the following year we're still working on the last ones.

D·1

Filing extension

Because we need work all year round, we can't complete every return before the regular deadlines (income tax 1 May, corporate income tax 1 June). The Dutch tax authorities have given us permission to apply for an extension, giving us an extra year to file our clients' returns — and we make use of it.

Would you rather have your return filed as soon as possible, or prefer to wait as long as you can? Let us know and we'll take it into account in the planning.

D·2

You are and remain responsible yourself

Even though you pay us for our services and may assume we do everything right, it's important to keep paying attention yourself. First: fortunately we've never had to deal with serious errors, but we're only human. You know your situation best; if you see something you're unsure about, tell us right away. We'll explain it, or correct it well in time.

Second: under Dutch law, you as the owner of the business are responsible for, among other things, the finances and the taxes that flow from them. We work as transparently as possible and explain anything you have questions about, so you stay in control of your own administration, annual accounts and tax returns.
